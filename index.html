Newsletter: My Journey in Germany

A Journey of Challenges, Growth, and Triumph

In March 2021, I took a bold step into the unknown as I embarked on my journey to Germany. I left my family and friends behind to pursue a Master's degree in Embedded Systems at the University of Freiburg. However, as much as I admired the field, I quickly realized it wasn't my calling...

A Bold Decision: Changing Course

It was a difficult decision, but I chose to leave the University of Freiburg and follow my instincts. I enrolled in the Software Engineering and Management program at Heilbronn University of Applied Sciences, where I felt a renewed sense of purpose. I thrived in an environment that combined both technical and management skills, and it was here that I discovered the fascinating world of data.

Discovering the Power of Data

My journey toward data analytics began when I was introduced to Intelligent Systems at Heilbronn University. I was captivated by the endless possibilities that data offered. From there, I immersed myself in learning everything I could about data management and analysis. Excel, SQL, Python, Power BI, R—I embraced it all, developing a strong foundation in data-driven decision-making.

Challenges and Growth: Working at ANEST IWATA EUROPE GmbH

As I honed my skills, I landed a role as a working student in database engineering at ANEST IWATA EUROPE GmbH. This opportunity was a game-changer. My performance as a student earned me an internship and, eventually, a full-time contract. From there, I never looked back.

I continued to push myself to learn new technologies while building my personal development journey. ANEST IWATA EUROPE GmbH gave me the platform to work on challenging projects and grow as a professional in data analytics and management.

Thesis Milestone: Exploring Collaborative Learning

One of my proudest achievements during my studies was my thesis. I conducted a qualitative and quantitative survey to explore the effectiveness of Collaborative Online International Learning (COIL) among first-year bachelor students. This work provided valuable insights into the collaborative learning process and its impact on international education.

Overcoming Adversity: Injuries and Isolation

But my time in Germany wasn't without challenges. Being far from home, I struggled with loneliness, cried alone, and battled injuries. After a knee operation, I had to fight daily to recover and continue my studies. Making friends was tough, and most of my attempts failed. But I persevered, relying on the support of my parents, my long-distance girlfriend (now my wife), my cousin brothers, friends, and ANEST IWATA EUROPE GmbH.

Gratitude and Looking Ahead

Finally, I completed my Master's degree with flying colors, and here I stand today, ready to face the world and make a difference. I owe my success to my parents for their sacrifices, my wife for her unwavering support, my cousins and friends, and my incredible team at ANEST IWATA EUROPE GmbH.

This journey has taught me resilience, the importance of support systems, and the value of following one's passion. I'm grateful for every experience, good and bad, that has shaped me into the person I am today. I look forward to standing tall, helping others, and achieving more in the world of data, software, and management.
